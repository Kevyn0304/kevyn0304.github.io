My childhood was a little complicated. I was born in Las Vegas, Nevada on March 4th, 2002. I went to preschool and first grade there. Then I moved to Mexicali, Baja California, Mexico, because my parents needed to sort some things out. I spent 3 years there, experiencing what primary school was like outside of the US. Then my family returned to Las Vegas, where I finished elementary school and started middle school. However, my parents were deported during winter break of 7th grade. From here on out, I lived in Mexicali, but crossed the border every day by foot to finish my education in Calexico, California, a little border town in the Imperial Valley, 2 hours East of San Diego. Here I completed middle school and high school. I then got admitted into UC Berkeley's College of Engineering to study Electrical Engineering & Computer Science (EECS).
